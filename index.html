Our Method

The Modern Fit Pilates Method

This method is built on flow and connection. Classes are designed to move the body as a whole—strengthening and stretching simultaneously while emphasizing deep core support.

Rather than pushing through movements, the focus is on feeling them, understanding where they originate, and moving with intention from the center outward.

The work is challenging, but always accessible, with personalized modifications in a small-group environment.

It's a traditional approach to Pilates, without the rigidity of classical or the extremes of high-intensity methods.

Our Story

Modern Fit Pilates

Alexis Interdonato is the founder of Modern Fit Pilates, established in 2022 after returning to her roots on Long Island from Los Angeles, California—where she trained and became a certified Pilates instructor.

An athlete from a young age, Alexis spent her childhood as a competitive figure skater, drawn to speed, agility, and the discipline required to master complex movement. Skating became more than a sport—it was an outlet, a source of strength and control during challenging and uncertain times. That early relationship with movement shaped her lifelong connection to fitness, both physically and mentally.

Throughout adulthood, fitness remained a powerful tool for clarity, resilience, and self-confidence. At 29, after moving to Los Angeles, Alexis discovered Pilates for the first time through the reformer. The method was completely unfamiliar—yet instantly transformative. Unlike traditional workouts focused on isolated movements, Pilates offered a continuous, flowing practice rooted in control, precision, and deep core connection. The experience felt intuitive, almost dance-like, echoing the grace and athleticism of her skating background.

Pilates taught Alexis how movement truly begins from the center—how strength, awareness, and control originate within. This understanding ultimately led her to pursue certification with the intention of teaching and sharing the method in a way that felt both authentic and empowering.

Elizabeth Photo

Elizabeth Szatkowski

Pilates Instructor

Elizabeth is a former competitive gymnast whose lifelong relationship with movement began at a young age. Years of high-level training left her with severe back trauma and chronic pain that followed her for more than 15 years. After trying countless forms of exercise, Pilates was the only method that truly healed her body. That experience inspired Elizabeth to become a Pilates teacher. She brings empathy, precision, and athletic intelligence to every session, creating classes that are challenging yet deeply supportive. Her goal is to help clients move out of pain, build strength, and reconnect with their bodies in a sustainable way.
